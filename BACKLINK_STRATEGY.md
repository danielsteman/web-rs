# Backlink Building Strategy for danielsteman.com

## Overview

Building quality backlinks is crucial for SEO. Here's a practical, actionable plan to get your site ranking higher for "Daniel Steman".

---

## 1. Social Media Sharing

### Strategy

Share your blog posts and site on social platforms where you already have profiles.

### Action Items

#### LinkedIn (High Priority)

- **Profile**: Update your LinkedIn profile bio to include: "Software engineer writing about tech at danielsteman.com"
- **Posts**: Every time you publish a blog post:
  1. Share it on LinkedIn with a brief summary
  2. Tag relevant technologies/topics
  3. Engage with comments
  4. Share in relevant LinkedIn groups (Rust, Software Engineering, etc.)
- **Articles**: Republish key insights as LinkedIn articles with a link back to your site

#### GitHub (High Priority)

- **Profile README**: Create/update `github.com/danielsteman/README.md` with:
  ```markdown
  ## Blog

  I write about software engineering at [danielsteman.com](https://www.danielsteman.com)
  ```
- **Project READMEs**: Add links to relevant blog posts in your project READMEs
- **Discussions**: Participate in GitHub Discussions and link to relevant posts when helpful

#### Stack Overflow (Medium Priority)

- **Profile**: Add your website URL to your Stack Overflow profile
- **Answers**: When answering questions, occasionally reference your blog posts if highly relevant
- **Be careful**: Don't spam - only link when it genuinely adds value

#### Twitter/X (If you have it)

- Share blog posts with relevant hashtags (#rustlang, #webdev, #softwareengineering)
- Engage with tech community discussions
- Retweet relevant content and add your perspective

## 2. Tech Community Contributions

### Strategy

Become an active, helpful member of tech communities. Build reputation first, then naturally share your content.

### Action Items

#### Reddit

- **Subreddits to target**:
  - `/r/rust` - Share Rust-related posts
  - `/r/programming` - Share general programming insights
  - `/r/webdev` - Share web development posts
  - `/r/softwareengineering` - Share engineering posts
- **Rules**:
  - Read subreddit rules first (some don't allow self-promotion)
  - Use "Show HN" or "I wrote about X" format
  - Engage genuinely - don't just drop links
  - Comment on others' posts too

#### Hacker News

- **How to post**: Submit your best posts to Hacker News
- **Format**: Use descriptive titles, not clickbait
- **Engage**: Respond to comments thoughtfully
- **Timing**: Post during US business hours for better visibility

#### Dev.to

- **Cross-post**: Republish your blog posts on Dev.to with canonical link back to your site
- **Engage**: Comment on others' posts, build community
- **Tags**: Use relevant tags (#rust, #webdev, #beginners)

#### Medium

- **Import tool**: Use Medium's import feature (preserves canonical link)
- **Publication**: Submit to relevant Medium publications
- **Engage**: Clap, comment, follow relevant writers

#### Hashnode

- **Cross-post**: Similar to Dev.to, republish with canonical links
- **Tags**: Use appropriate tags for discoverability

#### Rust Community

- **users.rust-lang.org**: Participate in discussions
- **Rust subreddit**: Share Rust-specific content
- **Rust Discord/Slack**: Help others, occasionally share relevant posts

## 3. Guest Posts & Collaborations

### Strategy

Write for established tech blogs/publications to get high-authority backlinks.

### Target Publications

#### High Authority Targets

1. **Dev.to** - Write original posts (not just cross-posts)
2. **Hashnode** - Contribute to their publications
3. **freeCodeCamp** - Submit tutorials (they accept contributions)
4. **Smashing Magazine** - Technical deep-dives
5. **CSS-Tricks** - Web development focused
6. **SitePoint** - Web development tutorials

#### How to Pitch

1. **Research**: Read their content, understand their style
2. **Proposal**: Email with:
   - Brief intro about yourself
   - Topic idea with outline
   - Why it's valuable to their audience
   - Link to your portfolio/blog
3. **Follow up**: Politely follow up after 1-2 weeks if no response

#### Content Ideas

- "Building a Serverless Blog with Rust and Axum"
- "HTMX vs React: A Practical Comparison"
- "SEO Optimization for Static Sites"
- "Rust for Web Development: Lessons Learned"

## 10. What NOT to Do

❌ **Don't**:

- Buy backlinks (Google penalizes this)
- Spam links in comments
- Use link farms or PBNs
- Over-optimize anchor text
- Ignore community rules
- Only self-promote without engaging

✅ **Do**:

- Build genuine relationships
- Provide value first
- Follow community guidelines
- Engage authentically
- Be patient (SEO takes time)
